Kubernetes rollout checklist

Use this before approving a Kubernetes rollout or rollback. Keep kubeconfig, cluster tokens, raw logs, secret manifests, private cluster endpoints, customer namespaces, internal URLs, and personal data out of public tools.

1. State the rollout scope

  • Namespace, workload kind/name, service, image, tag, and replica count
  • Customer or service impact scope
  • Rollout window and owner

2. Check strategy and availability

  • RollingUpdate, max surge, max unavailable, and expected available pods
  • PDB, disruption, maintenance window, or dependency constraints
  • Readiness, liveness, startup probes, and health-check behavior

3. Review change surfaces

  • ConfigMap, Secret, env var, volume, service, ingress, resources, HPA, affinity, tolerations, or selector changes
  • Validation path: rollout status, smoke tests, metrics, alerts, restart count, and logs owned by the team
  • Rollback path: revision, previous image/tag, approval owner, and rollback validation

4. Avoid unsafe claims and private data

  • Do not guarantee zero downtime or instant rollback unless that is explicitly validated.
  • Do not paste kubeconfig, service account tokens, Secret manifests, certificates, raw logs, cluster endpoints, or customer namespaces.
  • Use public-safe notes and scrubbed manifest excerpts only.
